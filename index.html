Heart facts

Leading cause

Heart disease is the leading cause of death worldwide, responsible for more than 17 million deaths each year.

Risks

High blood pressure, smoking, high cholesterol, obesity, diabetes, and a sedentary lifestyle are all major risk factors for heart disease.

Third heading

Heart disease can affect people of all ages, including children. Congenital heart defects are the most common birth defect, affecting around 1 in 100 newborns.

Medical Conditions and Increased Heart Disease Risk

Certain medical conditions, such as lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, and HIV/AIDS, can increase the risk of developing heart disease.
